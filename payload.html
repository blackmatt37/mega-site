Payload


The P-3C Orion will be carrying various instruments designed for collecting data. One of these instruments includes the Atlas sensor. This sensor has a three beam laser that will be used on ICESat-II and has improved elevation estimates. Other instruments that will be used consist of: DACOM, AVOCET, ACAM2, DLH, P-3C Strobe Data System, Snow Radar, and MCo RDS.


Spectrometers identify chemicals by sampling them through an inlet and running various tests against them to determine their composition, whereas remote sensing uses a variety of techniques to create a topographical mapping of a given area.


Langley Aerosol Research Group Experiment (LARGE) is a clister of instrumentation that provides precise measurements of aerosol character, aerosol levels, and traces.


And as all airborne laboratories, the P-3C Orion has a stock data system for basic navigational and geological data such as altitude and location.


The Diode Laser Hygrometer (DLH) is a tunable diode laser spectrometer which identifies water vapor.


Differential Absorption CO Measurement (DACOM) is a three channel tunable diode laser spectrometer that identifies carbon monoxide, methane, and dinitrogen monoxide.


Atmospheric Vertical Observations of CO2 in the Earth's Troposphere (AVOCET) is a CO2 identifier and tracer which records carbon dioxide levels.


Airborne Compact Atmospheric Mapper (ACAM) is a sensor that consists of two spectrographs and a video camera that records air quality and ocean color.


On the ground, the AErosol RObotic NETwork (AERONET) will also provide data about aerosol character and composition.


The airborne laboratory allows these sensors to work in synchrony and transport data via the INMARSAT and IRIDIUM satellite constellations which provide data uplink/downlink, internet, flight tracking, and instant messaging with the ground amongst other functions.
